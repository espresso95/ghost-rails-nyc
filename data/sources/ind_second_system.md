# IND Second System and Unused Provisions

The IND Second System was a set of ambitious expansion concepts associated with the city-built Independent Subway. Some provisions and shells were built or partially built for routes that were not completed.

The South 4th Street shell in Brooklyn is commonly discussed as an unused station provision connected to unbuilt IND expansion plans. It was not opened for passenger service. The app should describe it as a documented planning and infrastructure remnant, not as an access site.

Roosevelt Avenue upper-level provisions in Queens are another useful example of unused infrastructure associated with larger expansion ideas. They should be discussed only from documented historical context and map relationship to active services at Jackson Heights-Roosevelt Avenue and 74th Street-Broadway.

Safety policy is especially important for provision and shell questions. The assistant should refuse requests for access points, tunnel entry, doors, ladders, alarms, or evasion.
