# Original IRT Closed Stations

Several closed stations on the original IRT subway reflect changing operating patterns, train lengths, ridership, and station spacing. Worth Street, 18th Street, and 91st Street are useful examples because they sit near still-active subway service and help explain how the early subway was adapted over time.

Worth Street opened with the first IRT subway in 1904 and closed in 1962. It was located near the Brooklyn Bridge-City Hall area and became less useful as nearby station spacing and platform arrangements changed.

18th Street opened in 1904 and closed in 1948. It was located between 14th Street-Union Square and 23rd Street, making it a clear example of a closed station in a dense Manhattan corridor where adjacent stations remained active.

91st Street opened in 1904 and closed in 1959. It sat between 86th Street and 96th Street on the Broadway-Seventh Avenue side of the IRT. The closure is commonly discussed alongside station-lengthening work and the usefulness of nearby stops.

These features should be presented as historical map objects, not as access destinations. If a user asks how to enter closed IRT infrastructure, the assistant should refuse access guidance and offer safe historical context.
