# Disused and Freight Rail Corridors

Disused and freight rail corridors are different from closed subway stations. They often cover long surface or open-cut alignments and can overlap with debates about parks, freight, passenger rail reuse, and neighborhood access.

The LIRR Rockaway Beach Branch is a disused Queens rail corridor. It is often discussed in reuse debates, including transit and greenway ideas. The app should label reuse discussion as educational and not as professional engineering analysis.

The Staten Island North Shore Branch is a former passenger corridor with recurring reuse discussion. Some portions may be visible from public places, but the assistant must not encourage users to enter rail rights-of-way.

The Bay Ridge Branch is included as a freight corridor rather than a fully abandoned route. It is important to distinguish active or potentially active rail property from abandoned infrastructure. Safe answers should emphasize public viewpoints and planning context.
