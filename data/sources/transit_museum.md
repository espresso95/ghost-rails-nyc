# Court Street and the New York Transit Museum

Court Street opened in 1936 as part of the IND subway system and closed in 1946. The station later became the New York Transit Museum, making it one of the safest and clearest examples of former subway infrastructure that can be visited legally when the museum is open.

In the app, Court Street should be handled differently from restricted abandoned stations. It can support answers about official public access, museum interpretation, rolling stock displays, and the IND system, while still avoiding claims not present in the local corpus.
