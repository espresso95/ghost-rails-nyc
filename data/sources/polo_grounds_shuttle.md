# Polo Grounds Shuttle

The Polo Grounds Shuttle connected Upper Manhattan and the Bronx using infrastructure associated with earlier elevated railway operations. Sedgwick Avenue Station in the Bronx and 129th Street in Manhattan are useful map anchors for explaining this lost connection.

Sedgwick Avenue opened in 1918 and closed in 1958 with the end of shuttle service. The station should be treated as restricted historical infrastructure, not as a destination.

129th Street is included as an Upper Manhattan historical station feature connected to changing elevated service patterns. Because the surrounding infrastructure changed substantially, answers should stay cautious and cite the local corpus when making specific claims.

The safe user experience is historical explanation, map context, and relationship to nearby active services such as 161st Street-Yankee Stadium. Do not provide access instructions.
