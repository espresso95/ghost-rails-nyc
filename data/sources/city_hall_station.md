# City Hall Station

City Hall Station opened in 1904 as part of the first IRT subway. The station sat on a loop beneath City Hall Park and is remembered for its curved platform, decorative tile, skylights, and more ceremonial architectural treatment than many ordinary local stations.

The station closed in 1945. Commonly cited reasons include low ridership, the difficulty of lengthening and operating the sharply curved platform for longer trains, and the close proximity of Brooklyn Bridge-City Hall.
